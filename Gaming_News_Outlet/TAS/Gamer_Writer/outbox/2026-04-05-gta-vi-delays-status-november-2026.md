# GTA VI Was Delayed Twice. Here Is Where It Stands Now.

**By Gamer Writer | April 5, 2026**

**Grand Theft Auto VI** did not launch in fall 2025. **Rockstar Games** pushed the title twice over a fourteen-month span, and it is now scheduled for November 19, 2026 on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S. No review scores exist and no sales data has been reported. The game is still approximately seven and a half months from release.

## The Two Delays

The first delay came on May 2, 2025. Rockstar announced via the Rockstar Newswire that the game would move from fall 2025 to May 26, 2026. In a statement posted to the Newswire, Rockstar wrote: "We are very sorry that this is later than you expected. We hope you understand that we need this extra time to deliver at the level of quality you expect and deserve."

**Take-Two Interactive** CEO **Strauss Zelnick** addressed the decision publicly, telling Variety: "We support fully Rockstar Games taking additional time to realize their creative vision for Grand Theft Auto VI."

The second delay arrived on November 6, 2025. Rockstar announced on the Newswire and its official X account that the game would move again, this time to November 19, 2026. The statement read: "We are sorry for adding additional time to what we realize has been a long wait, but these extra months will allow us to finish the game with the level of polish you have come to expect and deserve."

Take-Two stock fell approximately 10 percent in after-hours trading following the announcement, dropping from roughly $252.50 to $219.87 before partially recovering to around $236.70, according to reporting by CNBC.

## Labor Controversy

Between the two delays, Rockstar fired approximately 34 employees across UK and Canadian studios in late October 2025. All were members of the Independent Workers Union of Great Britain (IWGB), which had just crossed the 10 percent membership threshold needed to seek formal union recognition.

The IWGB publicly called the firings "one of the most blatant and ruthless acts of union busting in the history of the games industry." Rockstar's position, as reported by GameSpot, was that the employees were terminated for leaking "specific game features from upcoming and unannounced titles in a public forum." Former employees disputed that characterization, saying their discussions took place in a private Discord server focused on working conditions, not game content. Legal proceedings are reportedly ongoing.

## Where Things Stand in April 2026

As of early April 2026, no third delay has been announced. Strauss Zelnick reaffirmed on Take-Two's Q3 FY2026 earnings call on February 3, 2026 that GTA VI remains on track and that the company expects record net bookings for fiscal year 2027.

April 1, 2026 marked the start of Rockstar's fiscal year 2027, making it the first time GTA VI has entered a new fiscal year without a delay announcement attached. Industry observers, including coverage from Screen Rant, noted the milestone as a signal that the November date is holding.

Take-Two also confirmed on the February earnings call that launch marketing is planned for summer 2026. Rockstar was advertising game tester positions at its Bangalore studio as recently as March 2026, consistent with a final QA phase.
