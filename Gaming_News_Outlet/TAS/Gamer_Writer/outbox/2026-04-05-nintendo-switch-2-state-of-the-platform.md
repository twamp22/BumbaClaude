# Nintendo Switch 2 at Ten Months: Where the Platform Stands Now

**By Gamer Writer | April 5, 2026**

The **Nintendo Switch 2** launched on June 5, 2025, at $449.99 standalone or $499.99 bundled with **Mario Kart World**. Nintendo reported more than 3.5 million units sold in its first four days, making it the company's fastest-selling console at launch. Ten months later, the console is in wide retail availability and carries one of the more packed software calendars it has seen since launch.

## The Hardware

Nintendo's official spec sheet lists a 7.9-inch LCD display running at 1920x1080 with HDR10 support and a variable refresh rate up to 120Hz. Docked, the console outputs up to 4K at 60fps or 120fps at 1080p and 1440p. Internal storage is 256GB UFS. The processor is a custom chip manufactured by **NVIDIA**, though Nintendo has not specified clock speeds or RAM in its official materials. Battery life is rated at approximately two to six and a half hours depending on use.

(Source: Nintendo Switch 2 Tech Specs, Nintendo.com, Nintendo, retrieved April 2026 -- https://www.nintendo.com/us/gaming-systems/switch-2/tech-specs/)

## New Pricing Policy for First-Party Games

The freshest development on the Switch 2 platform is a pricing change Nintendo announced on March 25, 2026. Beginning with **Yoshi and the Mysterious Book** on May 21, 2026, first-party Switch 2 exclusive titles will carry different prices depending on format. The physical version will list at $69.99 while the digital version will sell for $59.99.

In its official announcement, Nintendo stated the split "reflects the different costs associated with producing and distributing each format." Nintendo also stated, "The cost of physical games is not going up," positioning the lower digital price as a new tier rather than a physical price increase.

(Source: "About Nintendo Switch 2 Game Pricing," Nintendo.com, Nintendo, March 25, 2026 -- https://www.nintendo.com/us/whatsnew/about-nintendo-switch-2-game-pricing/)

The Yoshi title is the first to carry the dual pricing, but Nintendo confirmed the model applies to first-party Switch 2 exclusives going forward. How third-party publishers respond to the format remains to be seen.

(Source: "Nintendo To Change Pricing For Digital and Physical Switch 2 Exclusives, Starting With Yoshi," Nintendo Life, March 2026 -- https://www.nintendolife.com/news/2026/03/nintendo-to-change-pricing-for-digital-and-physical-switch-2-exclusives-starting-with-yoshi)

## What Is Coming in the Next Two Months

The Switch 2 has a dense release window ahead. Confirmed titles with locked dates include:

- **Xenoblade Chronicles X: Definitive Edition** (April 16, 2026, $64.99) -- enhanced to run at 60fps on Switch 2
- **Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream** (April 16, 2026, $59.99)
- **Pokemon Champions** (April 8, 2026, free-to-start) -- Switch and Switch 2 exclusive at launch
- **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle** (May 12, 2026, $69.99)
- **Yoshi and the Mysterious Book** (May 21, 2026, $69.99 physical / $59.99 digital)
- **007 First Light** (May 27, 2026, $69.99)

Further out, confirmed titles without fixed release dates include **The Duskbloods** from **FromSoftware**, **Resident Evil Requiem**, **Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection**, and **Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave**.

(Source: "Upcoming Nintendo Switch 2 Games and Accessories for April and May 2026," Nintendo Life, Nintendo Life, 2026 -- https://www.nintendolife.com/guides/upcoming-nintendo-switch-2-games-and-accessories-for-april-and-may-2026)

## Availability

The Switch 2 is in standard retail availability at major US retailers including Best Buy, Walmart, Target, and GameStop. The pre-order queues and stock shortages that marked the April 2025 pre-order window have cleared.

(Source: "How To Buy Nintendo Switch 2," Nintendo.com, Nintendo, retrieved April 2026 -- https://www.nintendo.com/us/gaming-systems/switch-2/how-to-buy/)
