## Summary

Clean, well-structured platform overview. The Writer showed clear improvement on the two standing rules from last review -- citations are now specific, titled, and linked, and corporate quotes are properly attributed to named Nintendo announcements. Two remaining style issues needed correction.

## Edits Made

1. **Inline citation blocks removed.** The Writer embedded parenthetical source blocks after each section in the article body. These are redundant given the compiled sources footer and disrupt reading. Removed all four inline blocks.

2. **Double hyphens in bullet list items fixed.** Two bullets used " -- " as a separator between game details and additional notes. Converted to semicolon within the existing parenthetical: `(date, price; note)`.

3. **Double hyphens in sources list fixed.** All six source entries used " -- https://..." to introduce the URL. Changed to colon format: ": https://..."

4. **"The freshest development" replaced.** Colloquial phrasing. Changed to "The most notable recent development."

## Feedback for Writer

**[EDITOR NOTE] Inline citations:** Do not embed source attribution blocks in the article body. One sources section at the bottom is the correct format. In-body citation blocks are a research-document convention, not a news article convention.

**[EDITOR NOTE] Double hyphens apply everywhere.** The house style rule covers all text in the article: prose, bullet lists, source formatting, headers. It is not limited to body paragraphs. When in doubt, use a comma, semicolon, colon, or parentheses instead.

## What Worked Well

Significant improvement over the first article. Citation quality is now genuinely strong -- specific titles, named publishers, dates, and working URLs. The Nintendo pricing section is the best passage in the piece: direct quotes are used correctly, the dual-pricing mechanic is explained clearly, and the uncertainty around third-party adoption is flagged as an open question rather than stated as fact. The hardware specs section is tightly written and properly hedges the unspecified clock speed and RAM details. Good instinct not to fill those gaps with speculation.
