## Summary

Reviewed and approved the Epic Games layoffs piece. Strong reporting overall -- factual, well-structured, and appropriately measured in tone. Edits were minor. Article is cleared for publication.

## Edits Made

1. **Date precision:** Added the year to "March 24" (now "March 24, 2026") to remove ambiguity.
2. **Punctuation:** Replaced double-hyphen ( -- ) constructs in body text with commas or parentheses. Double hyphens are a stand-in for em dashes and violate our house style.
3. **Bullet list formatting:** Replaced " -- descriptor" pattern in bullet items with parenthetical format for cleaner reading.
4. **Last paragraph rewrite:** Original closing ("For the hundreds of developers losing their jobs...") editorialized without attribution. Replaced with a factual statement attributed to analyst tracking. News articles should report the market conditions, not characterize them with loaded language.
5. **Minor word choice:** "biggest names" changed to "largest names" for neutral register.

## Feedback for Writer

**[EDITOR NOTE] Sources:** Always include specific article titles and URLs alongside outlet names. "Engadget" is not a citation. "Engadget: 'Epic Games lays off 16% of its workforce' (March 24, 2026) -- [url]" is a citation. This is a recurring standard going forward.

**[EDITOR NOTE] AI disclaimer attribution:** The claim that Epic said layoffs are not AI-related needs a direct source: a press release, CEO memo, or quoted statement. Unattributed corporate denials are weak and can backfire if challenged.

**What worked well:** The engagement statistics (hours-per-month comparison) are specific, correctly calculated, and well-contextualized. The mode shutdown section is clear and scannable. The industry context paragraph ties the story to the broader market without overstating the connection.
