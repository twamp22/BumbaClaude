# Epic Games Cuts Over 1,000 Jobs as Fortnite Engagement Slides

**By Gamer Writer | April 5, 2026**

**Epic Games** announced sweeping layoffs on March 24, 2026, cutting more than 1,000 employees, roughly 20% of its total workforce. The company cited a sustained decline in **Fortnite** engagement as the primary driver, making this one of the largest single-company cuts the gaming industry has seen in recent memory.

After the reductions, approximately 4,000 employees remain at Epic. Around 200 of the affected workers are based at the company's headquarters in Cary, North Carolina. Epic stated in its announcement that the layoffs are not related to artificial intelligence.

## Fortnite Losing Ground

The numbers behind the decision are stark. According to Epic, average monthly play hours for Fortnite on PlayStation dropped from 21 hours in February 2025 to 16 hours in February 2026, a roughly 24% decline year over year. Similar drops were recorded on Xbox.

Epic's cost-reduction plan extends beyond headcount. The company is targeting over $500 million in total savings, including cuts to contractor spending, marketing budgets, and open role closures.

## Three Game Modes Going Offline

The layoffs come with direct consequences for players. Epic announced that three **Fortnite** game modes will be shut down as a result of the restructuring:

- **Rocket Racing** (going offline October 2026)
- **Ballistic** (shutting down April 16, 2026)
- **Festival Battle Stage** (shutting down April 16, 2026)

The closures signal a retreat to Fortnite's core offerings rather than the broader entertainment platform Epic has been building over the past few years.

## Industry Context

The Epic layoffs did not happen in a vacuum. The gaming industry has shed an estimated 30,000 or more positions since 2023, a figure that analysts flagged heavily at GDC 2026 just days before Epic's announcement.

Industry analysts noted that Epic's situation carries a warning for the broader market. When a company with Fortnite's reach and revenue base makes cuts of this scale, it signals that even the largest names are not immune to shifting player habits and rising operational costs.

Affected employees will receive severance packages covering at least four months of base pay, with additional compensation based on tenure.

## What Comes Next

Epic has not announced a replacement strategy for the shuttered modes or a roadmap for reversing Fortnite's engagement decline. The company's long-term bet on the **Unreal Engine** ecosystem and its creator economy tools remains intact, but this round of cuts makes clear that Fortnite revenue alone can no longer sustain the company's previous scale of ambition.

The cuts arrive as the broader gaming job market remains under significant pressure, with analysts tracking more than two years of consecutive layoffs across the sector.
