# Retro Rewind Is the Surprise Hit Nobody Saw Coming

**By Staff Writer | April 5, 2026**

---

It doesn't happen often that a two-person indie studio launches a game on a Tuesday and immediately sits atop Steam's global sales chart. But that's exactly what Blood Pact Studios pulled off with *Retro Rewind - Video Store Simulator*, which has been one of the platform's top-ten sellers every day since its March 17 release -- debuting at number one.

The game puts players in charge of their own video rental store set in the early 1990s, a direct spiritual successor to the Blockbuster era. You stock shelves, process returns, rewind tapes, charge late fees, hire staff, and try to build the most popular rental destination in town. Seasons, weather, and holidays shift customer behavior throughout the year, and each new season brings its own festivals and special events.

## 100,000 Copies in Five Days

Blood Pact Studios confirmed the game crossed 100,000 units sold within its first five days on sale. Analyst Simon Carless of GameDiscoverCo has since estimated total sales approaching 200,000, placing it among the most successful new releases on Steam in March 2026. The game is sitting at "Overwhelmingly Positive" on Steam, with 95 percent of more than 3,100 user reviews rating it positively.

The game has received coverage from outlets including *The Hollywood Reporter*, an unusual crossover for a small indie simulator.

## 14,000 Fake Movies

One of the more remarkable design decisions in *Retro Rewind* is the scope of its fictional film catalog. The game ships with roughly 14,000 spoofed movie titles -- the vast majority generated via a proprietary database -- covering the range of schlocky genres that defined the rental-store era. The titles are parodies of real films: *Phantom Patrol*, for instance, is a send-up of *Ghostbusters*. It's the kind of detail that rewards browsing the shelves almost as much as managing the business.

## No Console Ports Coming

Demand for console versions has been loud enough that Blood Pact Studios addressed it directly in an official FAQ. The short answer: don't expect them. The studio's stated focus is on making the best possible PC game first, and they aren't committing to a console release.

## What's on the Roadmap

The developer has outlined a post-launch update plan that includes backroom storage, a VHS repair station, controller support, the ability to rent out video games alongside movies, and additional decorations and music. All planned updates are confirmed to be free -- no paid DLC on the roadmap.

*Retro Rewind - Video Store Simulator* is available now on Steam for PC.
