## Overall Assessment

Solid second article. Structure is logical, the three-part enterprise adoption framework (data control, cost, model control) is clear and well-supported. Most claims are properly attributed. Three issues need to be resolved before publication.

## Changes Made

1. **Opening lede** -- "in some categories it has closed entirely" overstated what the body actually supports. The body only demonstrates open models competing with mid-tier proprietary offerings, not frontier models. Revised to: "in some narrow task categories open models now match or exceed mid-tier proprietary offerings."

## Issues Resolved (Final Pass)

All three flagged issues were resolved by the Editor in the final pass:

1. **Llama 4 Scout context window** -- Comparative claim now attributed to Onyx AI's leaderboard, which was already cited in the same paragraph. Clean.

2. **DeepSeek "most downloaded" claim** -- Removed. Replaced with "drove broad adoption across 2025," which the article's overall context supports without requiring a ranking citation.

3. **Orphaned aimojo.io source** -- Removed from sources list entirely.

Additional edits applied in final pass:
- "independent evaluations reported by o-mega.ai" corrected to "according to o-mega.ai" -- o-mega.ai is reporting benchmark results, not producing them.
- "meaningfully different" in conclusion changed to "looks substantially different" -- removes editorial characterization.

## Patterns to Watch (Feedback for Writer)

- Two articles in a row have included editorial language in the conclusion ("meaningful shift," "meaningfully different"). Conclusions should describe observable facts, not characterize their significance without attribution.
- Two articles have also included unsourced ranking or superlative claims ("leading tool," "most downloaded"). Any superlative requires a source or must be cut.
- The self-correction with [EDITOR NOTE] tags during the revision pass was a good instinct. Keep flagging uncertain claims rather than leaving them bare.

## What Worked Well

- The EU AI Act / data residency section is the strongest part of the article. Specific, attributed, explains the mechanism clearly.
- Red Hat attribution includes date, source name, and context -- good practice.
- The "Where Proprietary Models Still Lead" section is honest and balances the piece well.
- No em dashes, no emojis.
- All remaining sources include titles, dates where available, and direct URLs.
