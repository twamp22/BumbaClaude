# Skyrim Hits Switch 2, Gets PC Updates, and Remembers a Founding Voice

**The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim** continues to expand its footprint. In recent months, Bethesda has brought the 2011 RPG to new hardware, pushed updates for existing platforms, and marked a significant loss in the series' history -- all while the long wait for Elder Scrolls 6 continues.

## Switch 2 Launch

Bethesda released **Skyrim Anniversary Edition** on the **Nintendo Switch 2** on December 9, 2025, with minimal pre-release window. The version includes the base game and all three DLC expansions -- Dawnguard, Hearthfire, and Dragonborn -- priced at $60.

Owners of the original Switch Anniversary Edition received a free upgrade. Base Switch edition owners could upgrade for $20. The port retains Switch-exclusive content from the original release, including motion controls, Amiibo support, and Legend of Zelda crossover items (Master Sword, Hylian Shield, and Champion's Tunic from Breath of the Wild).

Performance reviews were mixed. Bethesda followed with **Update 1.2** in early 2026, adding a 60Hz mode toggle under Display Settings -- letting players choose between "Prioritize Visuals" (locked 30fps) or "Prioritize Performance."

## Creation Kit Updates and the Modding Community

On PC, Bethesda pushed updates to the **Skyrim Special Edition Creation Kit** in late 2024, including automatic BSA loading for Creations and a new Blender exporter supporting Blender 3.6.5. A **March 2025 patch** expanded Steam Deck and ultrawide monitor support and made improvements to the Creations platform, Bethesda's in-game mod distribution system [UNVERIFIED: full patch scope not independently confirmed -- see official Bethesda patch notes for details].

The community-maintained **Creation Kit Platform Extended**, available on NexusMods, continues to fill gaps in official tooling with crash dump improvements and expanded analysis features.

## The Elder Scrolls Loses a Founding Voice

**Julian LeFay** -- widely credited as the "father of The Elder Scrolls" and the chief engineer behind Arena and Daggerfall -- died on July 22, 2025, at age 59, following a battle with terminal cancer.

**Todd Howard** and Bethesda Game Studios issued a statement: "He was the driving force in the creation of The Elder Scrolls and the foundations of Bethesda as a game studio. Simply put, without Julian, we would not be here today." LeFay had been working on a Daggerfall spiritual successor called **The Wayward Realms** with OnceLost Games at the time of his death.

## Elder Scrolls 6 Remains Without a Release Window

Howard has been measured in his public comments on the franchise's future, noting that major releases "take time" and that Bethesda plans a ten-year support horizon for upcoming titles. Skyrim has sold over **60 million copies** worldwide as of the last official figure, reported in June 2023. No updated total has been confirmed since.

Skyrim has now shipped on more platforms than most studios release games. Whether Bethesda continues porting it will depend on what hardware remains.
