# Skyrim Lands on Switch 2 and Mourns a Founding Father: Bethesda's Elder Scrolls Moment

**The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim** refuses to quietly retire. In the past several months, Bethesda has pushed the 2011 RPG onto new hardware, issued meaningful updates for existing platforms, and grappled with a significant loss in the series' history -- all while the long wait for Elder Scrolls 6 continues.

## A Surprise Switch 2 Launch

Bethesda dropped **Skyrim Anniversary Edition** on the **Nintendo Switch 2** on December 9, 2025, with no extended pre-release window. The version includes the base game and all three DLC expansions -- Dawnguard, Hearthfire, and Dragonborn -- priced at $60.

Owners of the Switch Anniversary Edition received a free upgrade to the Switch 2 version. Base Switch edition owners could pay $20 to upgrade. The port retains Switch-exclusive content carried over from the original release, including motion controls, Amiibo support, and the Legend of Zelda crossover items (Master Sword, Hylian Shield, and Champion's Tunic from Breath of the Wild).

Performance reviews were mixed. TechRadar described the port as rough around the edges but still absorbing, noting a reviewer sank 30 hours into it regardless. Bethesda followed up with **Update 1.2** in early 2026, adding a 60Hz mode toggle under Display Settings -- allowing players to choose between "Prioritize Visuals" (locked 30fps) or "Prioritize Performance."

## Creation Kit Updates and the Modding Community

On PC, Bethesda pushed updates to the **Skyrim Special Edition Creation Kit** in late 2024, including automatic BSA loading for Creations and a new Blender exporter with Blender 3.6.5 support. The community-maintained **Creation Kit Platform Extended**, available on NexusMods, continues to fill gaps in official tooling with crash dump improvements and expanded analysis features.

## The Elder Scrolls Loses a Founding Voice

The year's most somber note for the Elder Scrolls community came in July. **Julian LeFay** -- widely credited as the "father of The Elder Scrolls" and the chief engineer behind Arena and Daggerfall -- died on July 22, 2025, at age 59, following a battle with terminal cancer.

**Todd Howard** and Bethesda Game Studios issued a statement: "He was the driving force in the creation of The Elder Scrolls and the foundations of Bethesda as a game studio. Simply put, without Julian, we would not be here today." LeFay had been working on a Daggerfall spiritual successor called **The Wayward Realms** with OnceLost Games at the time of his death.

## Elder Scrolls 6 Remains Distant

Howard has been measured in his public comments on the franchise's future, noting that major releases "take time" and that Bethesda plans a ten-year support horizon for upcoming titles. Reports from early 2026 suggest **Elder Scrolls 6** is playable internally at the studio [UNVERIFIED: based on industry reporting, not an official Bethesda statement], though no release window has been announced. Bethesda is said to be developing future titles on Creation Engine 3.

Skyrim has sold over **60 million copies** worldwide as of the last official figure, reported in June 2023. No updated total has been confirmed since.

For a game that has now shipped on more platforms than most studios release games, the question is no longer whether Skyrim will come to the next platform -- but which one is left.
